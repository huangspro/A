EMPA‐Renal Tx 2019

Study characteristics

Methods

  • Study design: single centre, prospective, controlled, double‐blind, RCT

  • Study duration: 7 November 2016 to 28 June 2018

  • Study follow‐up period: 24 weeks

Participants

  • Country: Norway.

  • Setting: single centre in Oslo

  • Inclusion criteria: kidney transplant recipient transplanted more than 1 year ago and NODAT after transplantation (FBG ≥ 7.0 mmol/L and/or 2‐hour plasma glucose ≥11.1 mmol/L following an oral glucose tolerance test); ≥18 years with the following features: stable kidney function (< 20% deviation in SCr within last 2 months); stable immunosuppressive therapy ≥ 3 months before inclusion; signed informed consent and expected to cooperate.

  • Number: 49 recruited; 44 completed the study; 49 included in safety analyses

    • Treatment group (22); control group (22)

  • Median age and range (years): treatment group (63, 31 to 72); control group (59, 21 to 75)

  • Sex: (M/F): treatment group (17/5); control group (17/5)

  • Exclusion criteria: eGFR < 30 mL/min/1.73 m2; pregnant or nursing mothers; hypersensitivity to the active substance or to any of the excipients; any reason why, in the opinion of the investigator, the patient should not participate

Interventions

Treatment group

  • Empagliflozin: 10 mg/day

Control group

  • Placebo: tablet/day

Outcomes

Outcomes from study registry

  • Primary outcome

    • Weighted mean glucose estimated with continuous glucose monitoring

  • Secondary outcomes

    • FBG

    • 2‐hour glucose concentration

    • HbA1c

    • Body weight

    • Waist‐hip‐ratio

    • Body composition

    • Blood pressure

    • Arterial stiffness

    • Kidney function

  • Other outcomes

    • Incidence of abnormal trough levels of immunosuppressive drugs

    • Adverse event

    • Urinary glucose excretion

Outcomes for this systematic review

  • Primary outcomes

    • Transplant or graft survival: graft loss and rejection

    • Delayed graft function/dialysis within 7 days: not reported

    • HbA1c

    • FBG

    • Kidney function markers (including creatinine, eGFR, albuminuria): eGFR

  • Secondary outcomes

    • Death (any cause): not reported

    • Macrovascular events (cardiovascular death, non‐fatal MI, non‐fatal stroke, heart failure): not reported

    • Microvascular events (new or worsening kidney disease, doubling of SCr, retinopathy): not reported

    • Systolic and diastolic BP

    • Lipids (HDL, LDL, TG): not reported

    • Body weight

    • Safety/adverse events (including hypoglycaemia): hypoglycaemia, discontinuation of medication due to adverse events, other adverse events

    • QoL: not reported

Notes

Risk of bias

Bias

Authors' judgement

Support for judgement

Random sequence generation (selection bias)

Low risk

Quote: "The randomisation was performed by the R package “blockrand” using simple randomisation, one block, and equal probabilities of each sequence"

Allocation concealment (selection bias)

Low risk

Quote: "A person not directly involved in the practical implementation of the study performed the allocation. For each new patient entering the study, a study number was assigned with a prelisted drug package"

Blinding of participants and personnel (performance bias)
All outcomes

Low risk

Quote: "Both patient and investigator/study personnel were blinded with regards to study drug (active/placebo) until statistical analyses were performed"

Blinding of outcome assessment (detection bias)
All outcomes

Low risk

Quote: "Both patient and investigator/study personnel were blinded with regards to study drug (active/placebo) until statistical analyses were performed"

Incomplete outcome data (attrition bias)
All outcomes

Low risk

Five patients dropped out from the study. Two were from the empagliflozin group (2/24, 8.3%) due to repeated urinary tract infections and urosepsis. Three were from the placebo group (3/25, 12%), due to withdrawal of consent, colon cancer, and no longer fulfilling Post‐transplant diabetes criteria, respectively. However, data from these patient were included in the safety outcomes

Selective reporting (reporting bias)

Low risk

All preplanned outcomes were reported. Of note, data for the primary outcome of weighted mean glucose was not analysed or reported. However, this was due to a study technical error

Other bias

Low risk
