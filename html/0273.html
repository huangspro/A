Kharazmkia 2014

Study characteristics

Methods

  • Study design: parallel RCT

  • Study duration: September 2012 to May 2013

  • Study follow‐up period: 16 weeks

Participants

  • Country: Iran

  • Setting: University Hospital Transplant Unit

  • Inclusion criteria: diabetes, received kidney transplantation at least 1 month prior to study; stable graft function within the past 2 weeks

  • Number (randomised/analysed): treatment group (35/31); control group (36/31). However, 35 treatment and 36 control used for safety and followed‐up

  • Mean age ± SD (years): treatment group (50.2 ± 12.6); control group (54.8 ± 8.7)

  • Sex: (M/F): treatment group (24/7); control group (16/15)

  • Exclusion criteria: history of febrile episodes; hepatitis B or C infection; eGFR < 30 mL/min/1.73 m2 (based on MDRD at baseline); history of CHF (NYHA 2‐4); pregnancy; those who received steroid pulse or had an acute episode of allograft dysfunction (decrement of eGFR by at least 30 mL/min/1.73 m2) during follow‐up

Interventions

Treatment group

  • Pioglitazone (oral): 30 mg/day for 4 months

  • Insulin

Control group

  • Placebo

  • Insulin

Baseline immunosuppression

  • CSA‐based immunosuppressive protocol which consisted of MMF and prednisolone

Co‐interventions

  • All participants continued their insulin therapy

  • Glycaemic targets were pre‐prandial plasma glucose 5 to 7.2 mmol/L and HbA1c < 7%

Outcomes

Outcomes from the study registry

  • Primary outcomes

    • FBG

    • HbA1c

  • Secondary outcomes

    • Lipids

    • Inflammatory markers including C‐reactive protein, erythrocyte sedimentation rate, Interleukin‐18

    • SCr

    • Body weight

    • Safety/adverse events (including hypoglycaemia): lower extremity oedema, insomnia, transient elevation of liver enzyme levels 2 or more times the upper limits, and severe congestive heart failure

Outcomes for this systematic review

  • Primary outcomes

    • Transplant or graft survival: not reported

    • DGF/dialysis within 7 days: not reported

    • HbA1c

    • FBG: not reported

    • Kidney function markers (including creatinine, eGFR, albuminuria): creatinine

  • Secondary outcomes

    • Death (any cause): not reported

    • Macrovascular events (cardiovascular death, non‐fatal MI, non‐fatal stroke, heart failure): not reported

    • Microvascular events (new or worsening kidney disease, doubling of SCr, retinopathy): not reported

    • Systolic and diastolic BP: not reported

    • Lipids (HDL, LDL, TG)

    • Body weight

    • Safety/adverse events (including hypoglycaemia): lower extremity oedema, insomnia, transient elevation of liver enzyme levels ≥ 2 times the upper limits, and severe congestive heart failure

    • QoL: not reported

All outcomes

All outcomes

Low risk
