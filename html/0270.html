Haidinger 2010

Study characteristics

Methods

  • Study design: phase 2 RCT

  • Study duration: March 2009 to July 2012

  • Study follow‐up period: 16 weeks; 3 months of treatment, and primary and secondary outcomes reported at 3 months, and secondary outcomes at 4 months.

Participants

  • Country: Austria

  • Setting: single centre outpatient clinic, Department of Nephrology and Dialysis from Vienna General Hospital/Medical University of Vienna

  • Patients with NODAT, transplant vintage > 6 months; stable graft function (no clinically meaningful change in kidney function within the last 3 months) and informed consent

  • Number: treatment group (16); control group (16)

  • Mean age ± SD (years): treatment group (64.25 ± 27); control group (63 ± 8.4)

  • Sex: (M/F): treatment group (10/6); control group (14/2)

  • Exclusion criteria: prior history of T1DM or T2DM; pregnancy; severe kidney impairment with eGFR ≤ 30 mL/min/1.73 m2; severe liver impairment with AST and ALT 3x upper limit of normal

Interventions

Treatment group

  • Vildagliptin (oral): 50 mg/day for 3 months

Control group

  • Placebo

Baseline immunosuppression

  • TAC or CSA, MMF or MPA or AZA, and prednisolone

Co‐interventions

  • None

Outcomes

Outcomes from the study registry

  • Primary outcomes

    • 2‐hour blood glucose

  • Secondary outcomes

    • HbA1c

    • FBG

    • Kidney function markers including creatinine, (eGFR, albuminuria: eGFR

    • Systolic and diastolic BP

    • Lipids (HDL, LDL, TG)

    • Liver function parameters

    • BMI

    • Immunosuppressant serum level

    • Safety/adverse events (including hypoglycaemia)

Outcomes for this systematic review

  • Primary outcomes

    • Transplant or graft survival: reported on correspondence

    • DGF/dialysis within 7 days: not reported

    • HbA1c

    • FBG

    • Kidney function markers including creatinine, estimated glomerular filtration rate (eGFR), albuminuria: eGFR

  • Secondary outcomes

    • Death (any cause): reported on correspondence

    • Macrovascular events (cardiovascular death, non‐fatal MI, non‐fatal stroke, heart failure): cardiovascular events were reported on correspondence

    • Microvascular events (new or worsening kidney disease, doubling of SCr, retinopathy): not reported

    • Systolic and diastolic BP

    • Lipids (HDL, LDL, TG)

    • Body weight: not reported

    • Safety/adverse events (including hypoglycaemia): Severe hypoglycaemia (i.e. hypoglycaemia requiring third party assistance), discontinuation of medication due to adverse events, liver abnormalities/failure, angina pectoris, elevated pancreas enzymes, elevated triglycerides, cough, conjunctivitis, urinary tract infections, and leukopenia were reported.

    • QoL: not reported

Notes

  • Groups were well matched for nearly all baseline characteristics including gender, age, BMI (28.4 ± 4.5 versus 27.9 ± 6 kg/m2, P = 0.78), HbA1c (6.7 ± 0.73% versus 6.7 ± 0.82%, P = 0.95), eGFR (58.3 ± 16.3 versus. 53.6 ± 14.4 mL/min/1.73 m2, P = 0.40), time since transplantation (69.9 ± 63.9 versus 51.4 ± 47.2 months, P = 0.38) and prednisolone dose (3.6 ± 1.8 versus 4.1 ± 2.6 mg/d, P = 0.52). However, use of MPA (8 participants in the intervention versus 4 in the control arm, P = 0.02) and MMF (7 participants in the intervention versus 2 in the control arm, P = 0.049) at baseline was significantly different

  • Conflict of interest: none declared

  • Contact with authors: authors contacted

Bias

Low risk
